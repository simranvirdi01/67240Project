OUR STORY


In June 2011, Piedmont Community Services (PCS) received funding from The Harvest Foundation to implement the Community Recovery Program (CRP) which would be designed to reduce problems caused by substance abuse in Martinsville and Henry County, Virginia. After enrolling its first participants at the end of January 2012, the CRP has helped over 300 individuals maintain sobriety and find employment. In July 2014, CRP began expanding to Franklin County to provide the same services that have been provided to the Martinsville and Henry County comunities.



SERVICES


CRP primarily engages with individuals who have achieved at least three to six months of substance abuse recovery, helping them continue their recovery journey by addressing and offering assistance and supports in the areas of education, employment, finances, family, support/leisure, mental health, sobriety, spirituality, physical health, and housing.

The Community Recovery Program’s staff assist the participants by completing a vocational assessment that will assist in the development of vocational and recovery focused goals that the individuals would like to achieve while participating in the program.

Occupational Training Program (OPT)

The OPT will provide individuals with barriers to employment with the opportunity to develop useful vocational skills, positive work habits, and effective job-retention abilities that will lead to them overcoming vocational barriers in an actual work environment. The employment training will provide the individuals with the experience and the skills to enter a competitive job market.

Job Readiness Preparation

The Job Readiness Preparation will include the development of a resume, job searching, online- application, soft skills enhancement, and interview preparation.

Peer Recovery Specialist Support

The Peer Recovering Specialist support will consist of peer-to-peer coaching/mentoring development of a Wellness Plan, relapse prevention, guidance on participating in self-help programs, and referral to recovery related activities.

Residential Programs

The Landing (male recovery residence) and Serenity Cottage (female recovery residence) are safe and supportive environments for the participants of the Community Recovery Program. The goal of each residence is to create a community of individuals who are committed to recovery, an environment that will assist in the reduction of relapse, and a place that will provide continued support for the residents. It is a drug-free environment. The reasonable program fees will allow the members to prepare for the next phase of their lives.
