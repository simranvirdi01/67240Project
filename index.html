Community Recovery Program


The Community Recovery Program (CRP) assists individuals with a substance use and/or mental health disorder by providing vocational services through occupational training, linkage to employment opportunities, and referrals to other community resources that can lead to the individuals obtaining competitive employment and becoming productive members of the community.

Overall, the purpose of CRP is to (1) assist participants in gaining employment; (2) reconnect participants to the community by volunteering; 3) reduce the problems associated with substance use, (4) assist participants in locating housing in a safe environment; (5) provide guidance on physical and mental well-being; and (6) link participants to various community agencies and resources.


“Recovery is not only possible, but expected.”
